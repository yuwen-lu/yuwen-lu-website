Goalplay Video Coach

An AI-powered video coach APP for soccer goalkeepers.
In cooperation with Goalplay GmbH & Co. KG, part of the iOS practicum at Technical University of Munich ( iPraktikum 2019 ).

Real Industry Client Goalplay

Our project worked with the real client Goalplay, a company that combines the latest technology and the goalkeeper training founded by the legendary German goalkeeper Oliver Kahn.

Our team implemented industry-level Agile development principles and paid attention to the usability of the app.

We delivered our project through a Design Review and a Client Acceptance Test. Click on the links to watch the recordings of our presentations.

Machine Learning-Powered

Our app enabled goalkeepers to record their training videos and replay them, so that they can detect flaws in their practices from an objective view. We also offered personalized training plans for users. More than that, we integrated Machine Learning classification to detect the goalkeepers' performed training tasks (left/right dive, jump catch, etc.) and automatically calculate the completion status of their training plans. In the future, we can build other machine learning models to analyse the users' performance such as dive distance and jump height, to offer more professional feedback for their training.

As one developer in the team, I contributed to building the app using Swift and integrated the newly-released Apple ARKit motion detection module into our machine learning pipeline. I integrated the module to detect human's joint positions in the video frame and fed the data to our LSTM-based model, which conducted the classification.

A User-first Approach

As the Usability Manager of the team, I ensured our app meets the potential users' needs and implemented human-centered design principles.

Here are some activities we conducted as parts of our user-centered design:

  • User research (at a local football academy)
  • Defining user persona
  • Continuous usability evaluation using the provided framework CuuSE
  • Usability heuristics evaluation
  • User test

National Model United Nations

in New York City

I don't limit myself to a programmer, but also an interdisciplinary researcher and contributor in solving issues concerning all of us.

As a member of the National Model United Nations (NMUN) delegation of my home university, Dalian University of Technology, I participated in the NMUN conferences in New York continuously in 2018 and 2019.

General Assembly Fifth Committee (GA5) — 2018

In 2018, I joined with my partner, Tianran Liu, in the committee GA5 which focused on the administrative and budgetary matters of the UN. The country we were representing was the Republic of Latvia, one of the three Baltic countries. Here is our position paper. Our topics during the conference were:

We ended up focusing on the topic "Strengthening Compliance and Accountability in Field Missions with Special Regard to Sexual Exploitation and Abuse Cases" and discussed about the Sexual Exploitation and Abuse (SEA) cases in UN Field Missions. We worked on a victim-centered solution with a special focus on female victims. Our final draft resolution was passed by the committee and became part of the final resolution.

Commission on Population and Development (CPD) — 2019

In 2019, I, together with my partner Yuchen Lu, joined CPD, one of the ten Functional Commissions of the Economic and Social Council. Here is our position paper, where we discussed about the following topics for the conference:

  • Fulfilling the Nutritional Needs of the Ageing Population
  • Addressing the Effects of International Migration on Cities
  • Realizing SDG 3 by Ensuring Access to Essential Health-Care Services and Education

Our topic during the conference was decided by the body as the third one. That year, I became more familiar with the working style in the conference and the Rules of Procedus, thus more prepared. My partner and I contributed to two draft resolutions, focusing on the use of eHealth technology and mental healthcare. They were both adopted by the committee and formed the final resolution. It was recognized and archived by the real United Nations as part of the young generation's solution to current global issues.

Our hard work before and during the conference was also recognized by the dais and our peers with an Outstanding Delegate award and a Best Position Paper Award (the only two highest awards for individual delegates).
